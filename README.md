 📊 [Sip & Savor Beverage Co.] — Financial Model

![Dashboard Preview](screenshots/dashboard_overview.png)

## 📌 Project Summary
This project contains a comprehensive financial model and valuation for Sip & Savor Beverage Co., a company operating in the Fast-Moving Consumer Goods (FMCG) sector. The model provides a 5-year forecast (2024–2028) and includes detailed analysis of profitability, liquidity, and enterprise valuation.

## 🛠️ Tools Used
-Microsoft Excel: The core platform used for building the entire financial model.
-Advanced Formulae: Utilized for DCF valuation, WACC calculations, and automated financial statement linking.
-Pivot Tables & Power Query: Used for data transformation and synthesizing raw input data into meaningful summaries.
-Dynamic Dashboards: Created interactive visualizations to track KPIs and financial trends.
-Scenario Manager: Integrated to allow for "Best Case," "Base Case," and "Worst Case" financial forecasting.

## ✨ Key Features
1. Integrated Three-Statement Model
The core of the project is a fully dynamic link between the Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement. A change in a single assumption (like sales growth) automatically flows through all three statements, ensuring the model always balances.

2. Scenario Analysis & Stress Testing
- Base Case: The most likely outcome based on current trends.
- Best Case: Optimized growth and margin expansion.
- Worst Case: Stress-testing the business against market downturns or higher inflation.

3. Advanced DCF Valuation
 - A comprehensive Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) analysis is included to determine the company’s intrinsic value.
 - Free Cash Flow to the Firm (FCFF): Calculated by adjusting Net Income for non-cash items (Depreciation), CAPEX, and changes in Working Capital.
 - Terminal Value: Calculated using the Perpetual Growth Method (Long-term growth rate of 2.98%).
 - Equity vs. Enterprise Value: Clear bridge showing the transition from firm value to shareholder value.

4. Automated Financial Ratio Suite
The model automatically tracks and visualizes key performance indicators (KPIs) across four critical categories:

 - Profitability: GP Margin, OP Margin, NP Margin, and ROCE.
 -Liquidity: Current Ratio, Quick Ratio, and Cash Ratio.
 - Leverage: Debt-to-Equity and Interest Coverage.
 -Efficiency: Working capital cycles and asset turnover.

5. Dynamic Macro & Operating Assumptions
  -The "Input Data" sheet serves as a central "Control Room" for the model, featuring:
  -Inflation Adjustments: Automated indexing of costs based on forecasted inflation rates (26.3% to 14.0%).
  -Operating Drivers: Customizable credit sales, inventory days, and tax rate assumptions.

CAPEX Schedules: Detailed planning for acquisition and depreciation of Equipment, Furniture, Machines, and Delivery Trucks.

6. Executive Dashboard
A high-level visual summary that converts complex financial data into easy-to-read charts and tables, enabling stakeholders to quickly grasp the company's financial health and trajectory.

## 📂 Data Source
The data for the Britannia Juice Limited financial project was sourced from a comprehensive historical and operational dataset contained within a multi-sheet Excel workbook.

## 📈 Key Insights
- Margin Expansion: The model projects a steady increase in Gross Profit margins from 50.5% to 54.2% due to operational efficiencies and pricing strategies.
- Revenue Growth: Diversified product performance with Tropica Cola and Citrus Burst acting as primary revenue drivers.
- Solvency: The company maintains a consistent Debt-to-Equity ratio of 0.43 throughout the forecast period, indicating a balanced capital structure.
- Liquidity: Strong current ratios (peaking at 11.9x in 2025) suggest high short-term financial health, though these normalize toward 3.7x by 2028 as capital is deployed.
